An example of a disease that is caused by gene variations that come from both parents is sickle cell disease. When a parent has only one gene variation for sickle cell disease, the parent is a carrier for sickle cell but does not have the disease. When two parents are carriers for sickle cell disease, they have a 25% or a one in four chance of having a child with sickle cell disease.
