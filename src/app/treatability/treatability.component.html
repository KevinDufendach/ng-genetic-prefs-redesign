Treatability

Treatments can vary in how much they work. Some treatments can reverse or slow the disease. Other treatments help people live with a disease but cannot reverse or slow the disease.

An example of a disease that can sometimes be genetic is heart muscle disease. There are things a person with heart muscle disease can do to reverse or slow the disease. A person can make lifestyle changes, take medicine, have a pacemaker or any combination of the three treatments. Once treatments begin, they are usually lifelong.

Diseases that destroy the nerves can sometimes be genetic. Scientists have not yet found ways to reverse or slow genetic diseases that destroy the nerves. Treatments may help a person adapt or be more comfortable.
